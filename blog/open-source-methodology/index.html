Open Sourcing Security Management

March 2025

Why we're open sourcing our methodology to help build more effective security.

Why We’re Open Sourcing Our Security Management Methodology

We’ve been observing something troubling for a while now: despite increasing investments in security tools and technologies, many organizations still struggle to build effective security programs. The gap between spending and outcomes continues to widen, and we think there’s a fundamental reason why.

Security isn’t just about technology. It never has been.

At Peak Defence, we’ve spent years learning, iterating and developing methodology that balances People, Process, and Technology. Our approach incorporates four planning horizons that ensure both strategic vision and practical execution:

  • Lifetime Horizon: Setting the foundational security vision and principles that guide long-term decisions
  • Yearly Horizon: Establishing security objectives and maturity targets for sustainable growth
  • Quarterly Horizon: Breaking down annual goals into achievable milestones with clear success metrics
  • Monthly Horizon: Converting milestones into specific action plans with accountable owners

Then we try to translate strategy into daily execution, which hopefully makes our methodology particularly valuable. We apply simplified versions of proven productivity principles like the Pareto principle (focusing on the 20% of work that delivers 80% of security impact) and Getting Things Done (GTD) for security teams. This helps ensures that daily security work is both efficient and aligned with strategic objectives, rather than merely reactive.

Today, we’re announcing something that might seem counterintuitive for a security company: we’re open sourcing our methodology. We’re happy to share it, and look forward to iterating together with the community to make security management more effective for everyone.

Why Open Source Our “Secret Sauce”?

The decision to open source our methodology wasn’t the most obvious one. After all, we’ve invested countless hours refining our approach, testing it in real-world implementations, learning from successes and failures along the way. So why share it?

The answer is profoundly simple: security works better when knowledge is shared, not hoarded.

We believe that improving the security posture of organizations worldwide serves a greater purpose than maintaining proprietary methods. By making our methodology accessible to everyone, we aim to elevate the entire industry’s approach to security management.

The Problem We’re Addressing

Through our work with organizations of all sizes, we’ve consistently observed three critical challenges:

  1. Overwhelming complexity leading to security paralysis
  2. Disconnected systems and processes creating dangerous blind spots
  3. Human factors being overlooked in favor of technical solutions

These issues aren’t just inconveniences—they represent existential threats to effective security programs. When organizations can’t see the complete picture, when teams are drowning in alerts, and when human needs are ignored, even the most sophisticated security technologies fail to deliver their promised value.

Our methodology directly addresses these challenges by providing a structured yet flexible framework that organizations can adapt to their specific needs.

The Three Pillars of Our Approach

At the core of our methodology are three interconnected pillars that form the foundation of effective security management:

1. People-Focused

We recognize that humans are both the greatest strength and the greatest vulnerability in security. Our methodology embraces this reality by:

  • Designing workflows that reduce cognitive overload rather than adding to it
  • Empowering teams with AI-assisted decision support to make better choices faster
  • Creating clear guidance and prioritization to help people focus on what truly matters
  • Building systems that engage users rather than frustrating them

As one CISO we work with put it: “For the first time, my team isn’t fighting against our security tools—they’re actually eager to use them.”

2. Process-Centered

Strong security requires consistent, repeatable processes that adapt to changing conditions. Our methodology includes:

  • Standardized approaches that eliminate redundant tasks through intelligent automation
  • Real-time compliance monitoring across multiple frameworks
  • Proactive risk identification and mitigation workflows
  • Continuous improvement mechanisms driven by data, not guesswork

3. Technology Integration

Technology should simplify security work, not complicate it. We approach technology as an enabler that:

  • Integrates disparate security elements into a unified system
  • Uses AI to identify patterns and relationships traditional approaches miss
  • Automates routine tasks while highlighting areas requiring human judgment
  • Provides meaningful metrics that drive real security improvements

What Open Sourcing Means

Starting today, our complete methodology documentation is available on GitHub. This includes:

  • Implementation guides for organizations at different maturity levels
  • Process templates and workflows
  • Assessment frameworks and maturity models
  • Integration guides for connecting people, processes, and technology

We’re releasing this under a Creative Commons license that allows organizations to freely use and adapt our methodology while preventing commercial exploitation of the materials themselves.

Why Now?

The timing of this decision isn’t random. We’re at an inflection point in cybersecurity where:

  1. AI is transforming the security landscape, creating both opportunities and challenges
  2. Resource constraints are intensifying, forcing teams to do more with less
  3. Threat actors are becoming increasingly sophisticated, requiring more intelligent defensive approaches

By open sourcing our methodology now, we aim to help organizations navigate these challenges with approaches proven to work in real-world environments.

What This Means for Our Business

Some might wonder how open sourcing our methodology aligns with our business interests. We see it as perfectly aligned with our values and business model for several reasons:

  1. We believe in our technology. While our methodology provides the framework, our Wingman platform offers the most efficient implementation of these principles.

  2. We’re playing the long game. By helping elevate industry practices, we’re creating an ecosystem where our approach to security management becomes the standard.

  3. We practice radical transparency. This move aligns with our core value of openness and our belief that security thrives in the light, not in the shadows.

  4. Our virtual CISO services complement the methodology. Organizations that want expert guidance implementing these principles can still engage with our professional services team.

Join Us in Transforming Security Management

We believe that security management is at its best when it harmonizes people, processes, and technology into a cohesive whole. Too often, security programs overemphasize one element at the expense of the others, creating imbalances that ultimately undermine security effectiveness.

Our open-sourced methodology provides a framework for achieving this balance—but a framework is just the beginning. The real transformation happens when organizations make these principles their own, adapting them to their unique contexts and needs.

We invite security professionals, organizational leaders, and technology partners to:

  • Explore our methodology on GitHub
  • Provide feedback and contribute improvements
  • Share your experiences implementing these principles
  • Join our community of practice dedicated to advancing security management

Looking Forward

This is just the beginning of our open source journey. In the coming months, we’ll be hosting webinars, publishing case studies, and creating additional resources to help organizations implement this methodology effectively.

Security has always been a collective challenge requiring collective solutions. By open sourcing our methodology, we’re taking a meaningful step toward a future where effective security management is accessible to all organizations, not just those with the largest budgets or most sophisticated teams.

Because ultimately, we believe that security isn’t just what we do—it’s what we enable others to do. And that’s a mission worth sharing openly.

Peak Defence exists to transform how organizations manage security through the perfect integration of People, Process, and Technology. Our AI-powered Wingman platform and expert professional services help organizations build more resilient, effective security programs in an increasingly complex threat landscape.
