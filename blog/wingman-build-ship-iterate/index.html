Building and Shipping Wingman: Our Balanced Approach to GRC Innovation

In today’s fast-paced security landscape, creating innovative governance, risk, and compliance (GRC) solutions requires more than just technical expertise. At Peak Defence, our Wingman AI-powered GRC platform has transformed how organizations manage security, but the story of how we build and ship Wingman is just as important as what we’ve built.

Shaping Ideas, Not Just Features

Our approach to building Wingman begins long before any code is written. We’ve adopted a deliberate process of shaping work that ensures we’re solving real problems with meaningful solutions.

From Raw Ideas to Defined Solutions

When we identify potential enhancements for Wingman, we don’t immediately jump into development. Instead, we start by setting boundaries:

  1. Setting the appetite: We decide upfront how much time a solution is worth. Is this a two-week improvement or a six-week major feature?

  2. Narrowing the problem definition: Rather than building “what customers ask for,” we dig deeper to understand the underlying pain points. For example, when customers requested “more complex permission rules,” we discovered they actually needed better visibility into the impact of their actions. This led to a simpler, more elegant solution.

  3. Shaping the solution: We outline key elements at the right level of abstraction—concrete enough to guide implementation but abstract enough to leave room for creativity during building.

This shaping work happens on a separate track from our building cycles, allowing us to maintain a pipeline of well-defined projects ready for implementation.

Working in Fixed Cycles with Variable Scope

Rather than working in traditional two-week sprints, Wingman development happens in six-week cycles. This timeframe is deliberate:

  • Long enough to complete meaningful end-to-end features
  • Short enough that teams feel the pressure of the deadline from the beginning
  • Uninterrupted: Once committed to a project, teams work without distraction

The secret to our success is embracing variable scope within fixed time constraints. Instead of extending deadlines when challenges arise, we make tough decisions about what’s essential. We call this “scope hammering”—continuously asking what we can simplify or defer to meet our time commitment.

After each six-week cycle, we take a two-week “cool-down” period for reflection, bug fixing, and betting on what to build next.

Making Smart Bets, Not Building Backlogs

Unlike traditional development processes with endless backlogs, we make deliberate bets on what to build next:

“Backlogs are a big weight we don’t need to carry. The growing pile gives us a feeling like we’re always behind even though we’re not.”

During our betting table meetings, stakeholders review shaped pitches and decide which projects deserve attention in the upcoming cycle. This might be a “big batch” project occupying a team for the full six weeks or several “small batch” projects tackled sequentially.

By working this way, we ensure Wingman’s evolution is guided by strategic choices rather than the tyranny of accumulated ideas.

Building Vertically, Not Horizontally

When building new Wingman features, we avoid the common trap of completing all design work before starting development. Instead, we take a vertical slice approach:

  1. Get one piece done: Teams focus on delivering a complete, working element of the solution early—integrating design and code from day one.

  2. Mapping the scope: As work progresses, teams identify independent “scopes” that can be completed and shipped separately. This provides a language for discussing progress: “The Visibility Toggle is done, and we’re now working on Client Access.”

  3. Tracking real progress: We use hill charts to visualize progress for each scope, distinguishing between the “uphill” work of figuring things out and the “downhill” work of execution.

This approach ensures we’re not just checking off tasks but actually delivering integrated, working software throughout the cycle.

The Four Horizons of Wingman Development

While our six-week cycles provide structure for immediate execution, we balance this with a multi-horizon perspective:

Lifetime Horizon: Our Security Vision

Wingman isn’t just another GRC platform—it’s an expression of our enduring vision to make advanced security accessible to organizations of all sizes. This lifetime horizon guides our most fundamental decisions about which capabilities to invest in.

Yearly Horizon: Strategic Direction

Each year, we identify the most important capabilities for Wingman’s evolution. This might be enhancing our graph database technology for relationship mapping or improving our predictive analytics engine.

Quarterly Horizon: Tactical Implementation

Breaking down our yearly goals, we identify specific features and enhancements to develop across multiple six-week cycles, ensuring steady progress toward our strategic objectives.

Monthly Horizon: Operational Focus

At the monthly level, we ensure our development teams have clarity on immediate priorities, reviewing progress and making adjustments as needed.

A Culture That Empowers Innovation

Beyond our processes, it’s our culture that enables Wingman to continually evolve:

  • Team autonomy: We give complete responsibility to cross-functional teams rather than assigning individual tasks. This creates ownership and enables better problem-solving.

  • Done means deployed: Teams are expected to ship their work at the end of each cycle, creating real accountability for outcomes.

  • Focus on real problems: By continually asking “what’s the actual use case?” we ensure Wingman solves genuine customer pain points rather than implementing “nice to have” features.

Results That Speak Volumes

This balanced approach has yielded remarkable results for Wingman:

  • Faster time-to-market for new capabilities
  • Higher quality implementations with fewer bugs and rework
  • More innovative solutions that truly address customer needs
  • Sustainable pace that prevents burnout and maintains quality

The Path Forward

As we continue to develop Wingman, we’re staying true to our philosophy: “Trust your gut. Invest time in articulating and testing ideas. Be patient, move fast, but don’t rush.”

This blend of thoughtful shaping, deliberate betting, and empowered building enables us to maintain Wingman’s position as the leading AI-powered security management solution while ensuring we’re continuously delivering value to our customers.

By focusing on what matters most—solving real security management challenges with elegant, powerful solutions—we’ve transformed Wingman from a product into a true security partner for our customers.
